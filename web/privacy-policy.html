Privacy Policy

Effective 14 July 2026  ·  Last updated 14 July 2026

This policy explains what information the Katiya Station Restaurant Management System ("Katiya Station RMS", the "App") collects, why it collects it, and how it is handled. It applies to the Android app, the Windows desktop application, and the web application at katiyastation.com.

Katiya Station RMS is internal business software, not a consumer app. It is used by authorised staff of Katiya Station Restaurant & Bar to run day-to-day operations — taking orders, sending tickets to the kitchen, billing, inventory, and reporting. Accounts are created and issued by restaurant management. There is no public sign-up, and members of the general public are not intended users.

1. Who we are

Katiya Station Restaurant & Bar ("we", "us", "our") operates the App and acts as the controller of the information described below. Our backend is self-hosted and runs at api.katiyastation.com.

2. Information we collect

The App handles two distinct categories of information: data about the staff members who use the App, and data about the restaurant's guests, which staff enter in the course of serving them.

2.1 Staff account information

DataWhy it is collected
Name, role, and assigned branch To identify who is signed in, and to enforce role-based permissions — a waiter, a cashier, and a manager see different parts of the App.
Login credentials To authenticate you. Passwords are verified by our backend and are never stored on your device.
Phone number and profile photo Optional. Used only to identify staff within the restaurant's own records.
Attendance, shift, and salary records Employment administration, where the restaurant uses the App for this purpose.
Activity logs (audit trail) Records which staff account performed sensitive actions such as voiding a bill, applying a discount, or closing a shift. This is a standard accountability and anti-fraud control in point-of-sale systems.

2.2 Guest and order information entered by staff

To operate the restaurant, staff may record the following about guests. This information is entered by our staff, not collected automatically from any guest's device:

  • Guest name and phone number — used for table reservations, credit accounts, and the loyalty programme.
  • Order, billing, and payment records — items ordered, amounts, and payment method, as required for billing, accounting, and tax purposes.
  • Loyalty points and transaction history — where a guest participates in the loyalty programme.

2.3 Technical information

  • Push notification token — a device identifier issued by Firebase Cloud Messaging so we can deliver alerts to the right device (see section 6).
  • Network connection status — the App detects whether it is online or offline so it can queue work and sync later. This tells us only whether a connection exists, not your location or which network you are on.
  • Printer configuration — the local address of the receipt or kitchen printer you have paired, stored on your device only.

3. What we do not collect

We want to be specific about this, because it is unusually short for an app of this kind:

  • No location tracking. The App requests no location permission and does not collect GPS, coarse location, or location history.
  • No third-party analytics or advertising. The App contains no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, and no tracking pixels. We do not build advertising profiles, and we do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • No access to your contacts, camera, microphone, photos, call logs, or SMS.
  • No sale of personal data. We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to anyone, for any purpose.

4. How we use information

We use the information above solely to operate the restaurant. Specifically:

  • To authenticate staff and apply the correct role-based permissions.
  • To take orders, route kitchen tickets (KOT), produce bills, and print receipts.
  • To manage tables, reservations, inventory, purchases, suppliers, and expenses.
  • To run the loyalty and credit programmes for guests who opt in.
  • To generate sales, financial, and staffing reports for management.
  • To meet accounting, tax, and legal record-keeping obligations.
  • To secure the system, investigate suspected fraud or misuse, and maintain an audit trail.

We do not use this information for automated decision-making that produces legal effects, and we do not use it for marketing to third parties.

5. Data stored on your device

The App is designed to keep working when the internet connection drops — an ordinary condition in a busy restaurant. To do that, it stores some data locally on the device:

  • An offline cache of menu, table, and order data, held in a local database on the device so that service can continue during an outage and sync once connectivity returns.
  • Your session token, held in the operating system's encrypted secure storage (the Android Keystore on Android, and the equivalent facility on other platforms) so you are not forced to sign in repeatedly. Your password itself is never stored on the device.
  • App preferences, such as your printer settings and display options.

Signing out clears your session. Uninstalling the App removes all locally stored data from that device.

6. Push notifications

The App uses Firebase Cloud Messaging, a service provided by Google, to deliver operational alerts — for example, notifying the kitchen that a new ticket has arrived, or notifying a manager that a ticket has gone unacknowledged.

To route these messages, Firebase issues a registration token that identifies your device installation. We send this token to our backend so that alerts reach the correct device. Google processes this token as part of delivering the message. Google's handling of that data is governed by the Firebase Privacy and Security policy and the Google Privacy Policy.

Notification content is operational only. It never contains guest payment details. On Android 13 and later, notifications require your explicit permission, which you may decline or revoke at any time in your device settings — the rest of the App will continue to function.

7. Sharing and disclosure

We share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers. Google (Firebase Cloud Messaging) for push notification delivery, and our hosting provider for running the backend and web application. These providers process data on our instructions and for no independent purpose of their own.
  • Within the restaurant. Information is visible to other authorised staff strictly according to their role. A waiter cannot see financial reports; a kitchen account cannot see billing.
  • Legal obligations. Where we are required to disclose information by law, by a valid order of a court or competent authority, or to establish or defend legal claims.
  • Business transfer. If the restaurant is sold or reorganised, records may transfer to the successor, which will remain bound by a policy no less protective than this one.

We never sell personal information, and we do not share it with advertisers or data brokers.

8. Security

  • All traffic between the App and our backend is transmitted over HTTPS (TLS), encrypted in transit.
  • Session tokens are stored in the operating system's encrypted secure storage, not in plain text.
  • Access is governed by role-based permissions, so each staff account can reach only what its role requires.
  • Sensitive actions are recorded in an audit log attributable to a specific account.

No system can be guaranteed perfectly secure, and we do not claim otherwise. If we become aware of a breach that affects your personal information, we will notify affected individuals and any relevant authority as required by applicable law.

9. Data retention

We keep information only as long as it serves the purpose for which it was collected, or as long as the law requires — whichever is longer.

  • Sales, billing, and accounting records are retained for the period required by applicable tax and company law.
  • Staff records are retained for the duration of employment and for as long afterwards as employment and tax law requires.
  • Guest contact details held for loyalty or reservations are retained until the guest asks us to remove them, or until the record is no longer needed.
  • Push notification tokens are discarded when they expire or when the App is uninstalled.

10. Your rights and deletion

Subject to applicable law, you may ask us to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Delete information, where we are not legally required to keep it.
  • Object to or restrict a particular use of your information.

Account and data deletion

Because staff accounts are issued by restaurant management rather than through public sign-up, there is no self-service delete button inside the App. To have your account deactivated and your personal data deleted, contact us using the details in section 13, or speak to your branch manager. We will action verified requests within 30 days.

Please note that records we are legally obliged to retain — principally financial and tax records of completed transactions — cannot be deleted on request. Where that applies, we will delete everything else and tell you clearly what has been retained and why.

Guests who wish to be removed from the loyalty programme or have their contact details erased may make that request directly to the restaurant, and we will remove them from active records.

11. Children's privacy

The App is workplace software intended solely for adult employees of the restaurant. It is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we learn that we have inadvertently done so, we will delete it.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time — for example, if we add a feature that handles data differently. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. If a change is material, we will give notice within the App before it takes effect. Continued use of the App after a change takes effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
